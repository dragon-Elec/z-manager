What is ZRAM?

If you're new to Linux, here's why ZRAM matters for your system.

Compressed RAM, More Space

ZRAM creates a compressed block device in your RAM. Think of it as a "virtual swap" that lives entirely in memory and compresses data on the fly.

When your RAM gets full, instead of slowing down with a slow disk swap, ZRAM compresses memory pages to free up space — keeping your system snappy.

  • Faster than traditional swap (no disk I/O)
  • Reduces wear on SSDs and eMMC storage
  • Perfect for low-RAM systems (4GB-8GB)
  • Great for single-board computers (Raspberry Pi)
Apps & Data ~4.2 GB
ZRAM (Compressed) +1.8 GB equivalent
Free RAM ~2.0 GB
⚡ Up to 3x more usable memory!
